# SLIDE 2: PROBLEM STATEMENT OVERVIEW

## The Challenge

**Discovery and characterization of water-ice in the lunar South Polar Region** is a high-priority scientific and exploration objective, particularly for enabling sustained human presence on the Moon.

### Key Challenges:
- Identifying subsurface ice unambiguously
- Translating detections into actionable exploration strategies
- Navigating treacherous terrain in permanently shadowed regions
- Ensuring power availability for rover operations

### Why It Matters:
- **ISRU (In-Situ Resource Utilization):** Water can be converted to oxygen, fuel, and drinking water
- **Sustainable Human Presence:** Critical for long-term lunar missions
- **Scientific Discovery:** Understanding lunar volatile history

# SLIDE 3: OBJECTIVES

## Primary Objectives

### 1. Ice Detection & Mapping
Identify and map potential subsurface ice-bearing regions in lunar south polar PSRs, with emphasis on **'doubly shadowed craters'**

### 2. Radar Polarimetric Analysis
Utilize radar polarimetric signatures to distinguish ice-rich regions within 'doubly shadowed craters' from rough, rocky terrains

### 3. Landing Site Selection
Integrate terrain and illumination constraints to propose a scientifically viable and safe landing site near a 'doubly shadowed crater'

### 4. Path Planning
Design an optimal rover traverse path from the landing site to the target 'doubly shadowed crater'

### 5. Volume Estimation
Estimate the volume of subsurface ice within the top ~5 meters of lunar regolith
